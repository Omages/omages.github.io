An Object is Worth 64x64 Pixels:
Generating 3D Object via Image Diffusion

Xingguang Yan1, Han-Hung Lee1, Ziyu Wan2, Angel X. Chang1,3,
1Simon Fraser University 2City University of Hong Kong 3Canada-CIFAR AI Chair, Amii
arXiv Code

Abstract

We introduce a new approach for generating realistic 3D models with UV maps through a representation termed "Object Images," encapsulating surface geometry and appearance within a 64x64 pixel image. By converting complex 3D shapes into this manageable 2D format, we address the challenges of irregular connectivity and topological complexity inherent in polygonal meshes. This method allows us to leverage image generation models, such as Diffusion Transformers, for 3D shape generation. Evaluated on the ABO dataset, our approach achieves results comparable to recent 3D generative models in terms of geometric p-FID, while naturally supporting PBR materials.
